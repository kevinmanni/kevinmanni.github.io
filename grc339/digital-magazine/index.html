Welcome to Her Island

Caroline Polachek doesn’t just write songs — she builds worlds. In this feature, we step ashore into her surreal sonic island, where opera meets pop, and emotion erupts like lava. It’s theatrical, otherworldly, and entirely her own.

Living in the Fantasy World

Bladee’s legacy lies in turning vulnerability into vapor. As a pioneer of Drain Gang’s melodic, clouded sound, he helped shape a new wave of emotional, internet-born music — blending autotune, ambience, and existential drift into something haunting, holy, and deeply influential.

Immaterial

SOPHIE’s music transcended the physical, weaving together synthetic textures and emotional depth to create a legacy that feels both futuristic and deeply human — an immaterial force that continues to inspire.

Ethel Cain and the American Wasteland

Haunted churches, fading highways, and blood-soaked ballads — Ethel Cain’s world is gothic, mythic, and painfully real. This piece dives into how she turns trauma into storytelling, and Americana into something holy and haunted.

Dreams in Decay: Boards of Canada

Faded synths, warped childhood memories, and ghostly nostalgia — Boards of Canada make music that feels like watching home videos in a dream. This piece explores how they turn analog decay into something deeply emotional and eerily timeless.

Lies I Heard from Aphex Twin

Aphex Twin has claimed to own a tank, sleep two hours a week, and release hundreds of secret albums — all probably lies. But through this chaos, he’s built one of music’s most elusive and fascinating personas.

Choke Enough: Oklou’s New Dreamscape

Oklou’s Choke Enough captures a delicate balance between airy dream pop and sleek electronic production. With standout collaborations and intimate lyrics, the album invites listeners into a lush, introspective world.

Live at Revolution Hall: Adrianne Lenker’s Intimate Chronicle

Adrianne Lenker's Live at Revolution Hall captures the raw intimacy of her performances, blending fan favorites with unreleased tracks like "happiness." Recorded over three nights, this 43-track album offers a profound glimpse into her evolving artistry.

The Sound of Simulated Memory

Oneohtrix Point Never bends time and sound into surreal, digital landscapes. From eerie synths to glitchy nostalgia, his work blurs the line between memory and simulation — a soundtrack for the uncanny present.

Everything In Its Right Place

Radiohead turned alienation into art — fusing glitchy electronics, haunting lyrics, and existential dread. Their music still feels like waking up in a dream you can’t explain but never want to leave.

All Tomorrow’s Parties

The Velvet Underground’s “All Tomorrow’s Parties” is a cold, shimmering masterpiece — a song that turned detachment into art. With its droning piano, icy vocals, and hypnotic repetition, it became a blueprint for ethereal and experimental music. Its influence echoes in today’s dream pop, ambient, and avant-garde sounds, where melancholy meets elegance.

Living Is Easy With Eyes Closed

The Beatles’ “Strawberry Fields Forever” reshaped pop music with its dreamy production, surreal lyrics, and experimental sound. A key influence on today’s calm and ethereal artists, the track set the stage for music that blurs reality—echoes of its impact can be heard in everything from ambient pop to futuristic electronic ballads.
